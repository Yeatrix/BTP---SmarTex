Introduction

Identifying textile materials is a fundamental challenge. We explore AI to create a system that is fast, accurate, and non-destructive, addressing needs from quality control to large-scale recycling.

Quality Control

Ensuring material integrity and consistency in manufacturing.

Efficient Recycling

Automating the sorting of millions of tons of textile waste annually.

Consumer Protection

Fighting counterfeits and ensuring fair trade through reliable verification.

The Challenge & Our Solution

Traditional methods are slow, destructive, and subjective. Artificial Intelligence provides a powerful, modern alternative.

Limitations of Current Methods

Destructive & Time-Consuming

Chemical tests destroy samples, while microscopy is slow and laborious.

Subjective & Inconsistent

Manual identification relies on experience, leading to errors and inconsistency.

Why AI is the Answer

Accuracy & Objectivity

AI models achieve superhuman accuracy and provide consistent, unbiased results.

Non-Destructive & Fast

Analyzes materials using NIR spectroscopy in milliseconds without any damage.

Insights from Recent Research

Our work builds upon cutting-edge studies that combine spectroscopy with deep learning, demonstrating the high potential of this approach.

Shi & Sang (2025)

ModernTCN for Waste Textiles

Method: Used NIR spectroscopy on a large, custom dataset of 18 fabric types.

Model: Proposed Fabric-ModernTCN, a novel deep learning architecture for time series.

Key Finding: Achieved 93.28% accuracy and very high inference speed (390 FPS), outperforming other models.

Kainz et al. (2025)

Supervised & Unsupervised Learning

Method: Employed NIR Hyperspectral Imaging (HSI) to test generalization against color/structure variations.

Model: Compared a supervised 1D-CNN with an unsupervised Autoencoder.

Key Finding: The supervised 1D-CNN achieved perfect object-based accuracy, showing remarkable generalization.

Huang et al. (2022)

HSI and 1D-CNN

Method: Used HSI on 25 different commercial textile fibers.

Model: A 1D-CNN model compared against traditional ML (SVM, RF) and BPNN.

Key Finding: The 1D-CNN was superior, achieving 98.6% accuracy, significantly higher than Random Forest (91.4%).
